## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** No longer reads as generic AI slop on first glance. Spruce palette, Zilla Slab + Libre Franklin, ruled feature list, WhatsApp mockup, and flat surfaces distinguish it from the SaaS-default stack. Remaining tells: three identical pricing card shells and an external contact handoff that breaks brand continuity at conversion.

**Deterministic scan:** 0 warnings in `index.html`.

**Browser visualization:** Not run (no browser automation in this session). CLI scan only.

## Overall Impression

The page now communicates what Reception does and shows the WhatsApp proof before asking for commitment. Copy and visual hierarchy are trustworthy. The biggest remaining gap is trust at the moment of action: a fake demo number and a conversion path that leaves Reception for argo-navis.

## What's Working

1. **WhatsApp mockup beside the live demo.** Callers-eye view makes the product tangible without stock photography or icon grids.
2. **Header wayfinding.** Sticky wordmark, section links, and pricing CTA give mobile and desktop users orientation (mobile actions now align right after layout fix).
3. **Voice and typography.** Plain sentences, readable heading scale, and spruce-tinted neutrals match the local-service register.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Demo number is obviously placeholder
**What:** `+1 (555) 123-4567` reads as fake; Marcus (tradesperson persona) will not call it.
**Why it matters:** The demo section is the primary proof point. A fake number undermines the "show the real experience" principle.
**Fix:** Wire a real demo line or remove the call CTA until one exists; label honestly if it is coming soon.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable harden index.html`

### [P1] Conversion leaves Reception for argo-navis
**What:** Plan buttons open argo-navis.net contact form. Footer still exposes "argo-navis" in copyright.
**Why it matters:** Jordan may think they are signing up for a different company. Breaks peak-end at the pricing decision.
**Fix:** Reception-branded contact (subdomain or embedded form), or clearer copy that argo-navis operates Reception as the service provider.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable clarify index.html`

### [P2] No FAQ for common blockers
**What:** No answers for "Do I need WhatsApp?", "Does this work in my country?", or "How long does setup take?"
**Why it matters:** Riley (stress tester) and skeptical solo pros will bounce with unanswered objections.
**Fix:** Short FAQ section (4–5 questions) above footer or linked from pricing intro.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable onboard index.html`

### [P2] Pricing cards repeat the same structure
**What:** Three bordered cards with price block + bullet list + button; only Annual tier is visually differentiated.
**Why it matters:** Not hero-metric slop, but still template-shaped on long mobile scroll.
**Fix:** Consider ruled comparison list for features with one highlighted annual row, or reduce mobile repetition.
**Suggested command:** `/impeccable distill index.html`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer):** Finds Reception in the header quickly. At pricing, "Start Solo plan" opens another site without warning in the button label. May hesitate whether this is the same product.

**Marcus (Solo Electrician):** WhatsApp mockup and plain ROI copy build trust. Will not call the 555 demo. Wants a real number or a screenshot from a real business.

**Casey (Mobile):** Header CTA and menu now sit on the right. Pricing cards stack to a long scroll of similar bullets; usable but tedious.

## Minor Observations

- Tailwind CDN remains a dev convenience, not a production ship target.
- `View pricing plans` appears in header, hero, mobile drawer, and footer with slightly different labels.
- Annual "Recommended" badge is the only strong visual hierarchy moment in pricing.

## Questions to Consider

- What if the demo section hid the call button until a real number exists, and led with the mockup only?
- What if pricing opened a Reception-branded contact step instead of argo-navis.net?
- Would four FAQ answers remove more objections than another feature bullet?
