# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Solo independent professionals: tradespeople, consultants, therapists, and other one-person businesses who live on their phone. They are often on a job site, in a meeting, or with a client when the phone rings. Missing a call usually means the prospect moves on to the next name on Google. They are practical, time-poor, and skeptical of software that adds complexity.

## Product Purpose

Reception is a missed-call automation service. When a solo professional cannot answer, the caller receives an instant, personalized WhatsApp reply from their number. The landing page exists to explain the service plainly, let prospects experience it firsthand, and convert them into paid subscribers (Solo, Multi-Line, or Annual plans). Success is measured by sign-ups, not page views.

## Brand Personality

Reliable, trusted, human. Direct without being aggressive. The voice of a local service business you already know: practical, reassuring, no fluff. Not a SaaS vendor, not a sales pitch. Someone who shows up, does the job, and does not waste your time.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: cream backgrounds, gradient heroes, hero-metric blocks, endless icon-plus-heading card grids
- Aggressive sales pages: countdown timers, fake urgency, hype copy, pressure tactics
- Enterprise corporate aesthetic: navy-and-gold, stock photography, buzzword-heavy prose
- Playful startup aesthetic: illustrations, mascots, neon gradients, novelty for its own sake

## Design Principles

1. **Show the real experience.** The live demo call is the proof. Let prospects feel exactly what their missed callers will receive.
2. **Speak like a trusted local pro.** Plain language, specific outcomes, no marketing buzzwords. If a sentence could appear on any SaaS site, rewrite it.
3. **Prove value with plain math.** ROI should read like a conversation, not a dashboard metric block.
4. **Every word earns its place.** No restated headings, no intros that repeat the title, no filler reassurance.
5. **Clarity is the product.** Reception sells instant clarity for missed callers; the page itself should feel instantly clear.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline: sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 body text), keyboard-accessible interactive elements, visible focus states, semantic HTML structure, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any animation.
